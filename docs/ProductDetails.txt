Full Product Description:
TACC is a precision skin-prep formula designed for indoor climbing. it helps optimize tack, friction feel, and skin response on plastic and fiberglass holds. It’s not chalk, and it’s not lotion. TACC is built to prepare the skin for better contact and more consistent feel on modern indoor surfaces.

⸻
What TACC Is:
TACC is a pre session skin performance product for indoor climbers. It is applied by a few drops to the hands before climbing, rubbed in until the moisture feel is gone, then well rinsed with water before drying and chalking up. It is built for plastic and fiberglass holds, especially modern gym and comp-style surfaces.

⸻
What “Surface Lock” Means:
Surface Lock refers to the feeling of the skin settling into a more controlled, tacky, responsive state before climbing. It describes how TACC helps prepare the skin surface for better contact on plastic and fiberglass holds.

⸻

Who The Product Is For:
TACC is built for indoor climbers who care about performance and skin feel. It is especially for climbers training on plastic, fiberglass, board holds, slopers, pinches, and comp-style movement. It is for people who know when their skin feels dialed and when it doesn’t.

⸻
When Climbers Should Use It:
TACC should be used before a session, especially before hard projecting, board climbing, gym training, or comp-style climbing. It can also be reapplied between attempts if needed.

⸻
Application Instructions:
Apply 2-3 drops to hands before climbing. Rub it in over the hands until the moisture feel is gone (about 10+mins). Then rinse excess oils off with water only — do not use soap. Dry hands fully, then chalk up and climb. Reapply between attempts if needed.
⸻

TACC enhances your skin’s response to the hold surface. As a result, your hands may collect more chalk and residue over time. A quick rinse with water resets your skin, keeping clean hands throughout a session.

FAQ
Is TACC chalk?
No. Chalk absorbs moisture. TACC is a drop-applied formula that helps improve tack and friction feel.

Is TACC a lotion or moisturizer?
No. TACC is a pre-session skin-prep product, not a recovery product.

Will it make my hands greasy?
Not when used correctly. Rub it in, rinse excess oils off with water, dry fully, and chalk up.

Can I use it with chalk?
Yes. TACC is designed to work with chalk.

What surfaces is it for?
TACC is built specifically for plastic and fiberglass climbing holds.

When should I use it?
Before your session, and between attempts if needed.
